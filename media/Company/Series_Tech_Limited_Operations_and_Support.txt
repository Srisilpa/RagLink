Series Tech Limited Operations and Support


Purpose
This document describes the complete operations and support framework at Series Tech Limited, covering customer support, ticket management, incident management, escalation matrix, Service Level Agreements, knowledge management, procurement, vendor management, administration, office facilities, and employee support services. It is written so that any employee of Series Tech Limited can understand these operational areas fully without needing another document.


Scope
This document applies to employees in the Customer Success, IT Support, Administration, and Finance departments of Series Tech Limited, as well as project teams that provide post-go-live support to clients, across all Series Tech Limited locations.



Introduction
Series Tech Limited maintains a strong operations and support function to ensure clients receive timely assistance after their projects go live, and to ensure employees receive reliable internal support for daily operational needs across all offices in Hyderabad, Bangalore, Pune, Chennai, and Mumbai.


Customer Support
The Customer Success department of Series Tech Limited, based primarily in the Mumbai office, manages ongoing client relationships and coordinates support requests raised by clients after a project goes live. Customer Success works closely with project teams to ensure client issues are resolved promptly and that client satisfaction remains high throughout the engagement.


Ticket Management
All client support requests and internal employee support requests at Series Tech Limited are logged as tickets in the appropriate support system, whether the client support portal for client-facing issues or the internal IT support portal for employee technology issues. Every ticket is assigned a category, priority, and owner, and is tracked until resolution and closure confirmation.



Incident Management
An incident at Series Tech Limited refers to any unplanned event that disrupts or degrades a client s use of a Series Tech Limited-delivered system. Incidents are classified by severity, ranging from Severity 1, representing a complete service outage, to Severity 4, representing a minor issue with a workaround available. Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents trigger immediate engagement of the on-call engineer and the project lead.



Escalation Matrix
Series Tech Limited maintains a defined escalation matrix for support tickets and incidents. Tickets not resolved within the target resolution time are escalated automatically from the support engineer to the team lead, then to the project lead, and finally to the department head if still unresolved. Severity 1 incidents are escalated immediately and simultaneously to the project lead, the department head, and the Customer Success lead.



Service Level Agreements
Series Tech Limited commits to Service Level Agreements for each client project, defining target response times and resolution times based on incident severity. A typical Service Level Agreement at Series Tech Limited commits to responding to Severity 1 incidents within thirty minutes and providing an initial resolution or workaround within four hours, with longer target times for lower severity issues. Specific Service Level Agreement terms vary by client contract.



Knowledge Management
Series Tech Limited maintains a knowledge base for each client project, documenting common issues, their resolutions, and system runbooks used by support engineers. Employees are expected to contribute new knowledge base articles whenever they resolve a novel issue, so that similar issues can be resolved faster in the future.



Procurement
The Administration department of Series Tech Limited manages procurement of office supplies, equipment, and vendor services. Employees requiring procurement of items beyond standard office supplies must submit a procurement request through the internal administration portal, specifying business justification, which is reviewed by the Administration department and, for high-value purchases, approved by the Finance department.




Vendor Management
Series Tech Limited engages various vendors for services such as background verification, corporate travel booking, facility management, and specialized software licensing. The Administration and Finance departments jointly manage vendor contracts, ensuring vendor performance is reviewed periodically and that vendor agreements comply with Series Tech Limited s data security and confidentiality requirements.




Administration
The Administration department of Series Tech Limited manages office facilities, visitor registration, physical asset logistics, and general administrative support across the Hyderabad head office and the Bangalore, Pune, Chennai, and Mumbai branch offices. Administration also coordinates internal events and manages the office facilities budget for each location.



Office Facilities
Every Series Tech Limited office provides workstations, meeting rooms, a cafeteria, and recreational areas for employee wellbeing. Employees can book meeting rooms through the internal facilities booking system. Any facility issue, such as air conditioning malfunction or cafeteria equipment problems, should be reported to the Administration helpdesk.



Employee Support Services
Series Tech Limited provides employee support services including an employee assistance program offering confidential counseling support, an internal grievance redressal mechanism for workplace concerns, and a dedicated Human Resources helpdesk for policy questions. Employees can access these services through the internal HR portal or by contacting Human Resources directly.



Department Responsibilities
The Customer Success department is responsible for client relationship management and coordinating client support requests. The IT Support department is responsible for internal employee technology support. The Administration department is responsible for procurement, vendor management, and office facilities. The Finance department supports high-value procurement approval.


Policies
Series Tech Limited requires every support ticket to be logged in the appropriate system, regardless of how the request was initially received. Series Tech Limited requires Severity 1 incidents to be reported and escalated immediately, with no delay for internal confirmation. Series Tech Limited requires all vendor agreements to include data security and confidentiality clauses consistent with Series Tech Limited s cybersecurity standards.



Procedures
Clients raise support requests through the client support portal or by contacting their assigned Customer Success representative. Employees raise internal support requests through the internal IT support portal or the internal administration portal, depending on the nature of the request.



Approval Workflow
Standard procurement requests: Administration department review and approval. High-value procurement requests: Administration department review followed by Finance department approval. Incident escalation: automatic escalation through the defined escalation matrix based on severity and elapsed time.



Employee Responsibilities
Employees are responsible for logging support requests through the correct system, contributing to the knowledge base when resolving novel issues, and reporting facility issues to the Administration helpdesk promptly.



Manager Responsibilities
Managers and project leads are responsible for ensuring Service Level Agreement commitments are met for their client engagements, escalating unresolved tickets according to the escalation matrix, and supporting their team members in accessing employee support services when needed.



Examples
For example, a Severity 1 incident affecting Project Meridian, described in the Series Tech Limited Projects Guide, triggers immediate engagement of the on-call engineer and simultaneous escalation to the project lead, department head, and Customer Success lead, in line with the Series Tech Limited Service Level Agreement commitment to respond within thirty minutes.
Another example: an employee resolves a novel configuration issue on a client system and documents the resolution as a new knowledge base article, helping future support engineers resolve similar issues faster.



Employee Scenarios
Scenario 1: An employee needs to book a meeting room for a client presentation. The employee should use the internal facilities booking system to reserve the room in advance.
Scenario 2: A support ticket has not been resolved within its target resolution time. The ticket automatically escalates from the support engineer to the team lead, then the project lead, and finally the department head if still unresolved.
Scenario 3: An employee wants to procure a specialized software license for a client project. The employee submits a procurement request through the internal administration portal, which is reviewed by Administration and, if high-value, approved by Finance.




Common Mistakes
A common mistake is handling a client request informally over email instead of logging it as a ticket, which reduces visibility and can cause the request to be missed. Another common mistake is delaying the escalation of a Severity 1 incident while attempting to resolve it independently, when immediate escalation is required. A third common mistake is failing to document a novel issue resolution in the knowledge base, causing the same issue to take longer to resolve next time.

SERIES TECH LIMITED
OPERATIONS AND SUPPORT – EXTENDED EMPLOYEE KNOWLEDGE GUIDE
====================================================================

DOCUMENT PURPOSE

This document provides comprehensive operational guidance for employees
of Series Tech Limited. It covers internal and client support, ticket
management, incident management, escalation, Service Level Agreements,
procurement, vendor management, administration, facilities, employee
support, and common operational scenarios.

IMPORTANT SLA RULE

The standard Series Tech Limited SLA information in this document is
provided as a general operational guideline.

The actual SLA for a specific client project is determined by the signed
client contract and project-specific support agreement.

If a project-specific SLA conflicts with a standard Series Tech Limited
operational guideline, the applicable client contract and approved
project SLA take precedence.

Employees must check project-specific support documentation before
committing to response or resolution times.

====================================================================
1. TICKET PRIORITY AND SEVERITY MATRIX
====================================================================

Series Tech Limited classifies support tickets and incidents according
to business impact, urgency, number of affected users, and availability
of a workaround.

SEVERITY 1 – CRITICAL

A Severity 1 incident is a critical production issue that causes a
complete service outage or prevents a critical business function from
operating.

Examples include complete application unavailability, critical database
failure, complete production environment failure, or critical
infrastructure failure affecting essential business operations.

The target response time for a standard Series Tech Limited Severity 1
incident is thirty minutes. The target initial resolution or workaround
time is four hours, unless the specific client Service Level Agreement
defines a different commitment.

Severity 1 incidents require immediate engagement of the on-call
engineer, Project Lead, Department Head, and Customer Success Lead.

SEVERITY 2 – HIGH

A Severity 2 incident is a major issue that significantly affects
important functionality but does not result in a complete service
outage.

Examples include failure of a major application module, significant
performance degradation, or an important business function becoming
unavailable while other services continue to operate.

Severity 2 incidents require prompt investigation by the Support
Engineer and notification of the Team Lead and Project Lead.

SEVERITY 3 – MEDIUM

A Severity 3 incident affects limited functionality or a limited number
of users. A temporary workaround is normally available.

Examples include a non-critical application feature not working
correctly, a problem affecting a small group of users, or a recurring
issue with a documented workaround.

Severity 3 incidents are handled during normal support operations.

SEVERITY 4 – LOW

A Severity 4 issue is a minor problem, information request, cosmetic
issue, documentation issue, or non-urgent request.

Examples include minor user interface defects, general information
requests, documentation corrections, or minor configuration questions.

Severity 4 requests are handled according to normal support queue
priorities.

IMPORTANT SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION RULE

The final severity must be determined based on actual business impact
and the applicable project support agreement. If the business impact is
unclear, the Support Engineer should consult the Team Lead or Project
Lead.

The client-specific Service Level Agreement takes precedence over
general Series Tech Limited operational guidelines when different
response or resolution commitments apply.

====================================================================
2. TICKET LIFECYCLE
====================================================================

Every support request at Series Tech Limited follows a defined ticket
lifecycle.

STEP 1: TICKET CREATION

The requester creates a ticket through the appropriate support system.
Client issues must be logged through the client support portal or
assigned Customer Success representative. Employee technology issues
must be logged through the internal IT support portal. Administration
requests must be submitted through the internal Administration portal.

STEP 2: TICKET CATEGORIZATION

The support team categorizes the ticket as an application issue,
infrastructure issue, network issue, hardware issue, software request,
access request, procurement request, facility issue, or general
support request.

STEP 3: PRIORITY AND SEVERITY ASSIGNMENT

The support team evaluates business impact and urgency and assigns an
appropriate priority or severity.

STEP 4: TICKET ASSIGNMENT

The ticket is assigned to the appropriate Support Engineer, team, or
department. The assigned owner is responsible for investigation and
accurate ticket updates.

STEP 5: INVESTIGATION

The assigned team investigates the issue, reviews available logs and
documentation, communicates with the requester when information is
required, and identifies the likely cause.

STEP 6: RESOLUTION OR WORKAROUND

The support team provides a permanent resolution whenever possible. If
a permanent resolution requires additional development or maintenance,
a temporary workaround may be provided.

STEP 7: REQUESTER OR CLIENT CONFIRMATION

After applying the resolution, the support team requests confirmation
from the client or employee when appropriate.

STEP 8: TICKET CLOSURE

A ticket is closed after the issue has been resolved and confirmation
has been received or the applicable support process permits closure.
The ticket must contain sufficient information about the issue,
investigation, resolution, and communication.

STEP 9: KNOWLEDGE BASE UPDATE

When a ticket represents a new or important issue, the Support Engineer
should document the symptoms, cause, resolution steps, workaround, and
prevention recommendations in the appropriate Series Tech Limited
knowledge base.

REOPENING A TICKET

If the same issue occurs again shortly after closure, the requester may
request that the ticket be reopened or create a new ticket referencing
the previous ticket number.

3. DETAILED ESCALATION MATRIX
====================================================================

Series Tech Limited uses an escalation matrix to ensure that
unresolved or critical issues receive appropriate management attention.

LEVEL 1 – SUPPORT ENGINEER

The Support Engineer investigates the ticket, communicates with the
requester, and attempts to resolve the issue.

LEVEL 2 – TEAM LEAD

If the Support Engineer cannot resolve the issue within the applicable
target time or requires additional technical assistance, the issue is
escalated to the Team Lead.

LEVEL 3 – PROJECT LEAD

If the issue remains unresolved or creates significant project impact,
the Team Lead escalates the issue to the Project Lead.

LEVEL 4 – DEPARTMENT HEAD

If the issue remains unresolved, creates significant business impact,
or requires management intervention, the Project Lead escalates the
issue to the Department Head.

SEVERITY 1 ESCALATION

Severity 1 incidents are escalated immediately. The on-call engineer,
Project Lead, Department Head, and Customer Success Lead must be
informed according to the applicable project support process.

CLIENT ESCALATION

If a client reports repeated unresolved issues or expresses significant
dissatisfaction, the Customer Success Lead coordinates with the Project
Lead and Department Head.

SECURITY ESCALATION

Any suspected cybersecurity incident, data exposure, unauthorized
access, or security breach must be escalated immediately to the
Cybersecurity team. Employees must not delay security escalation while
attempting to investigate independently.

ESCALATION RULE

Escalation is based on severity, business impact, elapsed resolution
time, repeated failures, and contractual Service Level Agreement
requirements.

4. INCIDENT MANAGEMENT LIFECYCLE
====================================================================

Series Tech Limited follows a structured incident management lifecycle
to restore affected services quickly and minimize business disruption.

1. INCIDENT DETECTION

An incident may be detected by a client, employee, monitoring system,
Support Engineer, project team, or Customer Success representative.

2. INCIDENT REGISTRATION

The incident must be recorded in the appropriate support or incident
management system. The ticket must include the affected system, time
of occurrence, observed symptoms, affected users, and available
evidence.

3. SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION

The support team determines severity based on business impact and
urgency.

4. INCIDENT ASSIGNMENT

The incident is assigned to the appropriate Support Engineer or
technical team. For Severity 1 and Severity 2 incidents, the on-call
engineer and project leadership are engaged according to the applicable
support process.

5. INITIAL INVESTIGATION

The technical team investigates the incident to identify the immediate
cause and determine the extent of impact.

6. STAKEHOLDER COMMUNICATION

For major incidents, the Project Lead or Customer Success team
communicates appropriate updates to affected stakeholders. Updates
should include current impact, investigation status, workaround,
expected next update, and service restoration status when available.

7. MITIGATION AND WORKAROUND

The support team attempts to reduce business impact by applying a
temporary workaround or mitigation where possible.

8. SERVICE RESTORATION

The technical team restores the affected service and verifies normal
operation.

9. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

For significant incidents, the responsible technical team performs a
Root Cause Analysis to identify the underlying technical or process
failure and document corrective and preventive actions.

10. POST-INCIDENT REVIEW

The Project Lead and relevant technical teams review significant
incidents to identify lessons learned and prevent recurrence.

11. KNOWLEDGE BASE UPDATE

The final resolution, root cause, workaround, and preventive actions
should be documented in the Series Tech Limited knowledge base when
useful for future support activities.

5. PROCUREMENT CATEGORIES AND APPROVAL GUIDELINES
====================================================================

Series Tech Limited procurement requests are categorized according to
the type of purchase and business purpose.

STANDARD OFFICE SUPPLIES

Includes stationery, printing materials, meeting supplies, and routine
office requirements. These requests are reviewed by Administration.

IT EQUIPMENT

Includes laptops, desktop computers, monitors, keyboards, docking
stations, and other approved technology equipment. Requests require
business justification and may require approval from the employee's
manager, IT Support, Administration, or Finance.

SOFTWARE LICENSES

Requests must include software name, business purpose, number of users,
project or department, expected cost, and license duration.

CLOUD SERVICES

Requests must identify the cloud platform, estimated usage, expected
cost, project name, business purpose, and responsible technical owner.
Cloud procurement must follow Series Tech Limited security and access
requirements.

CLIENT PROJECT PURCHASES

Requests must include project name, project code, business
justification, and expected project benefit. Additional client
approval may be required for client-chargeable purchases.

EMERGENCY PURCHASES

Emergency purchases may be requested when an urgent business or
operational requirement cannot wait for the standard procurement
process. Emergency purchases require documented justification and
appropriate post-purchase review.

PROCUREMENT APPROVAL WORKFLOW

1. Employee or project team identifies the requirement.
2. Employee submits a procurement request through the internal
   Administration portal.
3. Request includes business justification, item details, estimated
   cost, and required date.
4. Administration reviews the request.
5. Finance approval is obtained when required.
6. Approved purchase is processed through the authorized procurement
   process.
7. Requester receives confirmation of procurement status.

IMPORTANT PROCUREMENT RULE

Employees must not purchase company equipment, software, or services
independently and expect reimbursement unless prior approval has been
obtained according to applicable procurement and finance policies.

7. ADDITIONAL EMPLOYEE SCENARIOS
====================================================================

SCENARIO 4: CRITICAL PRODUCTION OUTAGE

A client reports that the entire production application is
unavailable. The Support Engineer creates or updates a Severity 1
incident ticket, notifies the on-call engineer, and follows the
immediate escalation process. The Project Lead, Department Head, and
Customer Success Lead are informed according to the applicable project
support process.

SCENARIO 5: EMPLOYEE LAPTOP FAILURE

An employee's company laptop stops working during an active project.
The employee creates an IT Support ticket and provides device details
and a description of the problem. The employee informs the Team Lead if
the problem prevents urgent project work. IT Support determines
whether repair, replacement, or temporary equipment is required.

SCENARIO 6: SOFTWARE LICENSE REQUEST

A Software Engineer requires specialized software for a client
project. The employee submits a procurement request containing the
software name, business justification, project name, number of users,
and expected cost. Administration reviews the request and obtains
additional approval when required.

SCENARIO 7: REPEATED CLIENT ISSUE

A client reports that the same technical problem has occurred multiple
times. The support team reviews previous tickets, identifies whether
the incidents have a common root cause, and escalates the issue to the
Project Lead if a permanent solution is required.

SCENARIO 8: UNRESOLVED TICKET

An employee has submitted an internal IT ticket, but the issue remains
unresolved beyond the applicable target time. The ticket is reviewed
for escalation according to the support escalation process.

SCENARIO 9: OFFICE AIR CONDITIONING PROBLEM

An employee notices that the air conditioning in a meeting room is not
working. The employee reports the issue to the Administration Helpdesk
with the office location and meeting room number. Administration
coordinates with the appropriate facility vendor.

SCENARIO 10: NEW KNOWLEDGE BASE ARTICLE

A Support Engineer resolves a previously undocumented configuration
issue. The engineer documents the symptoms, root cause, resolution
steps, and workaround in the project knowledge base.

SCENARIO 11: VENDOR SERVICE FAILURE

A facility management vendor fails to provide a contracted service.
Administration records the issue, communicates with the vendor, and
evaluates vendor performance. Repeated failures may be escalated
through vendor management.

SCENARIO 12: WORKPLACE GRIEVANCE

An employee has a workplace concern that cannot be resolved through
normal management communication. The employee may use the internal
grievance redressal mechanism or contact Human Resources.

SCENARIO 13: SECURITY INCIDENT

An employee suspects that company credentials have been compromised.
The employee must immediately report the suspected security incident
to the Cybersecurity team and follow the cybersecurity incident
reporting procedure.

SCENARIO 14: EMERGENCY PROCUREMENT

A critical project requires an urgent replacement component that
cannot wait for the standard procurement timeline. The project team
submits an emergency procurement request with detailed justification.
Administration reviews the request and obtains required financial
approval.

SCENARIO 15: CLIENT SUPPORT REQUEST RECEIVED BY EMAIL

A client sends a technical support request directly to an employee's
email. The employee must ensure that the request is logged in the
appropriate client support system rather than handled only through
email. This ensures proper tracking, ownership, SLA monitoring, and
escalation.

8. ADDITIONAL FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
====================================================================

31. What should I do if a client sends a support request directly to
my email?

The request should be logged in the appropriate client support system
so it can be assigned, tracked, monitored against the applicable SLA,
and closed properly.

32. What is a Severity 1 incident?

A Severity 1 incident is a critical production issue that causes a
complete service outage or prevents a critical business function from
operating.

33. What is a Severity 2 incident?

A Severity 2 incident is a major issue that significantly affects
important functionality but does not result in a complete service
outage.

34. What is a Severity 3 incident?

A Severity 3 incident is a medium-impact issue affecting limited
functionality or a limited number of users, usually with a workaround
available.

35. What is a Severity 4 issue?

A Severity 4 issue is a low-impact issue such as a minor defect,
information request, documentation issue, or non-urgent request.

36. Who decides the severity of an incident?

The support team determines severity based on business impact and
urgency. The Team Lead or Project Lead may be consulted when severity
is unclear.

37. Can the severity of an incident change?

Yes. Incident severity may be increased or decreased if business
impact changes during investigation.

38. What happens after I create a support ticket?

The ticket is categorized, assigned a priority or severity, assigned
to an owner, investigated, resolved or provided with a workaround,
confirmed by the requester when appropriate, and then closed.

39. Can I reopen a closed ticket?

If the same issue occurs again shortly after closure, the requester may
request that the ticket be reopened or create a new ticket referencing
the previous ticket.

40. What happens if a ticket is not resolved on time?

The ticket is escalated according to the Series Tech Limited
escalation matrix and the applicable Service Level Agreement.

41. Who handles a critical production outage?

The on-call engineer and relevant project support team handle the
technical response, while the Project Lead, Department Head, and
Customer Success Lead are engaged according to the applicable
escalation process.

42. What should I do if I do not know the correct ticket severity?

Create the ticket with as much information as possible and consult the
support team or Team Lead for appropriate severity classification.

43. What information should I include in a support ticket?

Include a clear description of the problem, affected system, time of
occurrence, affected users, error messages, screenshots when
available, business impact, and steps that reproduce the issue.

44. What should I do if a production issue has no workaround?

The issue should be treated according to its actual business impact.
If it meets Severity 1 criteria, immediate escalation is required.

45. Who should I contact for a laptop problem?

Contact the IT Support department through the internal IT support
portal.

46. Who should I contact for an office facility problem?

Contact the Administration Helpdesk and provide the office location
and details of the facility issue.

47. Who should I contact for a software license?

Submit a procurement request through the internal Administration portal
with the required business justification and project information.

48. Can I buy software independently and claim reimbursement later?

Employees should not independently purchase company software or
services and expect reimbursement unless prior approval has been
obtained according to applicable procurement and finance policies.

49. What information is required for a procurement request?

The request should include the item or service name, business
justification, estimated cost, required date, department or project,
and other information required by the procurement process.

50. Who handles high-value procurement?

Administration performs the initial review, and Finance provides
financial approval when required.

51. What should I do if a vendor does not provide an agreed service?

Report the issue to Administration or the responsible vendor management
team so the vendor can be contacted and the issue tracked.

52. Who manages vendor contracts?

Administration and Finance jointly manage vendor contracts according
to their respective responsibilities.

53. What happens after a major incident is resolved?

The responsible team restores the service, verifies normal operation,
performs Root Cause Analysis when required, conducts a post-incident
review, and updates the knowledge base.

54. What is Root Cause Analysis?

Root Cause Analysis is a structured investigation used to identify the
underlying reason an incident occurred and define corrective and
preventive actions.

55. Who communicates with clients during major incidents?

The Project Lead and Customer Success team coordinate client
communication according to the applicable project support process.

56. Why should Support Engineers update the knowledge base?

Knowledge base updates allow future Support Engineers to quickly find
documented solutions, workarounds, and troubleshooting procedures for
recurring or similar problems.

57. Where should I report a suspected cybersecurity incident?

Employees must immediately report suspected cybersecurity incidents to
the Cybersecurity team according to the Series Tech Limited
Cybersecurity Policy.

58. Can I delay reporting a security incident until I confirm that a
breach occurred?

No. Suspected security incidents must be reported immediately.
Employees should not delay reporting while attempting to independently
confirm the incident.

59. What should I do if I have a workplace grievance?

Employees may use the internal grievance redressal mechanism or
contact Human Resources according to the applicable company policy.

60. Where can I find the resolution for a previously reported technical
issue?

Employees should search the appropriate project knowledge base or
internal knowledge management system for documented issues,
resolutions, workarounds, and runbooks.
