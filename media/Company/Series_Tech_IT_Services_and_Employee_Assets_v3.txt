Series Tech Limited IT Services and Employee Assets
Full Enterprise Operational Policy, Asset Governance & Systems Manual
Version 3.0 (July 2026)

================================================================================
1. PURPOSE & SCOPE
================================================================================
This document provides the complete, authoritative operational reference for IT services, infrastructure management, hardware asset lifecycle, software licensing, identity management, remote access, endpoint security, network rules, IT change management, and employee offboarding processes at Series Tech Limited.

SCOPE:
This policy applies to all full-time employees, part-time staff, interns, contractors, and third-party personnel who are issued Series Tech Limited IT assets or granted access to corporate networks, cloud platforms, or client environments across all operational hubs, including Hyderabad, Bangalore, Pune, Chennai, Mumbai, and hybrid remote workforces.

================================================================================
2. LAPTOP ALLOCATION & WORKSTATION TIER MATRIX
================================================================================
Series Tech Limited issues role-tailored computing hardware to ensure maximum developer velocity and operational performance.

Tiers of Hardware Allocation:
- Developer & Cloud Tier (Software Development, AI, DevOps, Data Engineering):
  High-performance workstations (Apple MacBook Pro 16" M-Series / Dell XPS 15, 32GB-64GB RAM, 1TB NVMe SSD). Configured for running local Docker containers, Kubernetes minikubes, and machine learning models.
- Core & Administrative Tier (HR, Finance, Operations, Business Development):
  Standard enterprise laptops (Dell Latitude / Lenovo ThinkPad, 16GB RAM, 512GB SSD). Configured for productivity applications and cloud SaaS platforms.
- Specialized Lab Workstations (QA Testing, High-Performance Compute):
  Dedicated desktop workstations allocated at regional hubs (e.g., Chennai QA labs, Bangalore AI research center) equipped with multi-GPU acceleration or isolated lab network interfaces. Desktop allocation requires manager approval and IT Infrastructure sign-off.

Hardware Protection Policies:
1. Tamper Prohibition: Opening laptop casings, replacing internal memory/storage, or disabling hardware switches voids warranty and violates corporate policy.
2. Endpoint Agent Safeguards: The pre-installed Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) agent (CrowdStrike / Defender for Endpoint) must remain active at all times. Tampering with or stopping security services leads to immediate access revocation.
3. Dual-Screen & Peripheral Allocation: Employees are eligible for 1x 27-inch 4K Monitor, ergonomic wireless keyboard/mouse, and noise-canceling headset upon request via the IT Portal.

================================================================================
3. IT ASSET INVENTORY & LIFECYCLE TRACKING
================================================================================
Series Tech Limited tracks every hardware asset throughout its complete operational lifecycle:

Asset Tracking & Tagging:
- Every physical device (laptop, desktop, monitor, mobile device) is assigned a unique Asset ID barcode and logged alongside its serial number in the central IT Asset Management System (ITAMS).
- Asset status transitions through defined states: In Stock → Assigned → Maintenance/Repair → Lost/Stolen → Retired/Decommissioned.

Asset Transfer & Ownership Rules:
- Assets are strictly linked to an employee's corporate ID. Peer-to-peer asset transfers without formal IT re-assignment through the portal are strictly prohibited.
- Annual Asset Audits: IT Support conducts mandatory annual hardware verification audits across all offices and remote teams. Employees must verify hardware serial numbers via automated agent check-ins.

================================================================================
4. DEVICE REPLACEMENT & REFRESH POLICY
================================================================================
- Standard Refresh Cycle: Laptops and primary workstations are refreshed every 3 to 4 years to maintain optimal hardware performance and battery efficiency.
- Battery & Performance Refresh Criteria: Devices exhibiting unrepairable hardware failures, battery capacity loss exceeding 35%, or persistent thermal throttling are eligible for expedited replacement upon IT diagnostic verification.
- Temporary Loaner Process: If an employee's device requires hardware repair, a temporary loaner laptop with an identical software baseline is dispatched within 24 hours (P1/P2 issues).
- Lost/Stolen Device Protocol: Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately to IT Support and Cybersecurity to initiate emergency remote wipe via MDM. Replacement hardware is issued upon filing an official incident report.

================================================================================
5. ENDPOINT MANAGEMENT, PATCHING & COMPLIANCE
================================================================================
All company-managed endpoints must maintain active disk encryption, endpoint protection, host firewall, and automatic security updates. Devices failing compliance checks may be quarantined from corporate network access.

Endpoint Compliance Requirements:
- Disk Encryption: Full-disk encryption (BitLocker / FileVault) is mandatory on all endpoints. Encryption keys are securely escrowed in enterprise secret vaults.
- Automatic Patching: OS security patches and third-party updates are auto-deployed weekly. Critical zero-day vulnerability patches must be applied within 48 hours of release.
- Screen Lock Timeout: Workstations automatically lock after 5 minutes of inactivity. Biometric or complex password authentication is required to unlock.
- USB & Peripheral Restrictions: Mass storage USB drives are blocked by default to prevent data exfiltration and malware introduction.

================================================================================
6. DATA BACKUP & ENDPOINT RECOVERY
================================================================================
- Approved Storage Locations: All work deliverables, source code, and project documentation must be stored in approved cloud locations (Google Workspace / OneDrive / Enterprise GitHub).
- Local Storage Limitations: Local laptop storage is considered ephemeral and unbacked. Storing non-backed-up critical client data on local C: or root drives is prohibited.
- Device Migration & Data Recovery: During laptop refresh or replacement, automated profile migration tools transfer browser bookmarks, user profiles, and cloud-synced documents within 2 hours.

================================================================================
7. EMPLOYEE OFFBOARDING & ACCESS REVOCATION
================================================================================
The IT access offboarding lifecycle is fully synchronized with HR status changes:
- Access Revocation Timeline: Upon effective termination or resignation time, single sign-on (SSO), VPN, email, JIRA, and Git repository access are revoked immediately (< 15 minutes).
- Device MDM Lock & Remote Wipe: Unreturned or remote offboarding laptops are locked via MDM and issued a cryptographic remote wipe command upon clearance completion.
- Physical Security & Badge Deactivation: Office access badges are deactivated at the end of the last working day.
- Data Ownership Transfer: The offboarded employee's manager is granted temporary read-only access to relevant cloud drive files for 30 days to complete business handover.

================================================================================
8. MOBILE DEVICE & BYOD POLICY
================================================================================
Personal devices may not access restricted corporate or client environments unless explicitly authorized and enrolled in the approved MDM/BYOD security program.

- MDM Containerization: Enrolled mobile devices utilize containerized workspaces separating corporate email, Slack, and calendars from personal applications and photos.
- Remote Wipe Scope: Corporate remote wipe commands affect only the secure business container; personal photos, messages, and apps on BYOD devices remain untouched.
- Security Baselines: Enrolled mobile devices must maintain active device pin/biometric locks, OS encryption, and up-to-date security patches. Rooted or jailbroken devices are strictly blocked.

================================================================================
9. NETWORK, WI-FI & REMOTE CONNECTIVITY
================================================================================
- Corporate & Guest Wi-Fi: Corporate Wi-Fi (SeriesTech-Secure) uses WPA3-Enterprise with 802.1X certificate authentication. Guest Wi-Fi (SeriesTech-Guest) is isolated from internal servers.
- Public Wi-Fi Restrictions: Employees connecting company laptops to public Wi-Fi networks (airports, cafes, hotels) must keep the corporate VPN enabled with active EDR controls.
- Network Segmentation: Development, Staging, and Production network segments are isolated using micro-segmentation and firewalls. Rogue access points, unauthorized routers, and personal Wi-Fi hotspots are prohibited in corporate offices.

================================================================================
10. SOFTWARE CATALOG & LICENSING COMPLIANCE
================================================================================
Software installation is governed by strict licensing, security, and approval workflows.

10.1 Approved Self-Service Software Catalog:
Engineers can install pre-vetted tools directly via the Series Tech Software Hub without supervisor approval:
- IDEs & Editors: VS Code, IntelliJ IDEA Community, PyCharm Community, Eclipse.
- Utilities & Containers: Docker Desktop (Enterprise Licensed), Git, Postman, Node.js, Python, OpenJDK.
- Collaboration: Slack, Microsoft Teams, Zoom, Google Workspace.

10.2 Licensing Rules & Piracy Prohibition:
- Piracy Ban: Cracked, pirated, or unauthorized software is strictly prohibited. Violation leads to immediate disciplinary action.
- Open-Source Compliance: Open-source software with permissive licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD) is permitted; copyleft licenses (GPL v3) require legal review before inclusion in client deliverables.
- License Reclamation: Unused software licenses are auto-reclaimed after 60 days of inactivity.

11.1 IT Change Management Workflow:
- Standard Changes: Pre-approved low-risk changes (e.g., standard software deployment, patch updates) executed during routine windows.

12. IT & OPERATIONS DECISION MATRIX
================================================================================
Direct, retrievable operational guidance for common IT questions:

Question: Can I use my personal laptop for official work?
Answer: No. Only company-issued devices managed under corporate MDM can connect to internal systems or client environments.

Question: What should I do if my laptop is stolen or lost?
Answer: Report it immediately to IT Support and Cybersecurity via telephone or emergency portal so remote wipe commands can be executed.

Question: Who approves non-catalog software requests with cost implications?
Answer: The Reporting Manager (for budget approval) followed by IT Support and Cybersecurity (for technical and risk vetting).

Question: Can I share my VPN or network credentials with family members?
Answer: No. Sharing credentials with anyone is strictly prohibited and constitutes a severe security breach.

Question: How quickly are critical (P1) hardware or laptop issues addressed?
Answer: Critical issues blocking work are prioritized for resolution within 4 working hours.

Question: Can employees connect company laptops to public Wi-Fi?
Answer: Yes, only when necessary and with the corporate VPN enabled and endpoint security controls active.

Question: Can employees install pirated or unauthorized software?
Answer: No. Pirated, cracked, or unauthorized software is strictly prohibited.

Question: How does Series Tech Limited track employee IT assets?
Answer: Every asset is assigned a unique Asset ID and serial number linked to the employee's corporate identity in the central ITAMS system.

Question: What happens to corporate data on mobile devices during offboarding?
Answer: The MDM system issues a selective wipe command removing only corporate data, email, and apps, leaving personal data untouched.
